Apply for Chapter Leader Position

Girl Develop It Chapter Leaders are dedicated individuals who are responsible for running the local chapters. They are focused on growing a sustainable membership base that furthers Girl Develop It’s mission to provide affordable and judgment-free opportunities for women from diverse backgrounds interested in learning web and software development.

There are many benefits that come with being a Chapter Leader — including joining a network of more than 200 talented, industry-leading technologists who lead our 50+ chapters across the US! We’re really excited and honored that you’ve chosen to join the Girl Develop It team!

As Chapter Leaders, we oversee all aspects of GDI Boulder as well as create a long-term vision for the chapter. We are here to set the tone for each and every GDI Boulder event - which means we must work hard to be friendly and welcoming to new & current members, we must encourage collaboration while discouraging negative behaviors, and sometimes we must make decisions that will impact of our chapter for better or for worse.

As CL’s, we also get the joy of seeing members grow into the type of people they’ve worked so hard to become. We have the opportunity to provide them with the skills they need to move forward through learning events, classes, connections, etc. We provide the structure and the environment for each and every one of our members to find lifelong friends, precious mentors & allies, and the strength to carry on.

What we do can at times be very difficult, but it is also immensely rewarding.

Chapter Leader responsibilities include:

  • Schedule and organize in-person classes, workshop and social events
  • Locate qualified Instructors (paid) and Teaching Assistants (volunteer)
  • Adhere to responsibilities and policies set forth by Girl Develop It including, but not limited to: curriculum, class/event fee structure and branding/marketing guidelines.
  • Maintain clear communication with GDI headquarters:, including
    • Providing monthly progress updates
    • Maintaining an open dialogue concerning issues within the local chapter
    • Tracking in-kind support to your chapter
    • Getting approval for new Chapter Leaders joining your local team
  • Support local partnership development efforts on behalf of the chapter
    • Revenue from classes and events is the primary source of chapter funding
    • Local partnerships include in-kind donations such as: classroom/event space, food, beverage
  • Report to GDI headquarters if you will no longer be able to fulfill your role as Chapter Leader or if you need to step back for a period of time
    • Please allow an adequate amount of time for us to find a replacement
